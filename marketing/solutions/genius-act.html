US Stablecoin Regulation Under the GENIUS Act

Compliance infrastructure for stablecoin issuers operating under the Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for US Stablecoins Act.

Overview

The First Federal Stablecoin Framework

The GENIUS Act establishes the first comprehensive US federal framework for payment stablecoin issuers. It mandates 1:1 reserve backing with qualifying assets, regular attestation by registered public accounting firms, and prohibition of algorithmic stablecoins.

Safeheld provides the automated verification infrastructure that stablecoin issuers need to demonstrate continuous compliance with GENIUS Act reserve and reporting requirements.

Regulation

GENIUS Act Key Requirements

Core regulatory obligations that Safeheld addresses for payment stablecoin issuers.

1:1 Backing

All payment stablecoins must be backed by qualifying reserves equal to or exceeding outstanding supply.

Monthly Attestation

Reserve adequacy must be attested monthly by a registered public accounting firm.

Qualifying Assets

Reserves limited to US currency, short-term Treasuries, Fed reserves, and qualifying repo agreements.

Redemption at Par

Issuers must honour redemption requests at face value within specified timeframes.

Prohibition on Algorithmic

Unbacked or algorithmic stablecoins are prohibited under the Act.

Insolvency Priority

Stablecoin holders have priority claim on reserves in the event of issuer insolvency.
