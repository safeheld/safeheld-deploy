MiCA

Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation

Verification infrastructure for CASPs, EMT issuers, and ART issuers under the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation.

Overview

The MiCA Regulatory Framework

MiCA establishes the first comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto-asset service providers across the European Union. It introduces specific obligations for three categories of regulated entity: crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) must segregate client assets from proprietary holdings under Article 70; e-money token (EMT) issuers must maintain reserves under Title IV; and asset-referenced token (ART) issuers must meet reserve composition and diversification requirements under Title III.

Beyond asset safeguarding, MiCA imposes prudential requirements on authorised CASPs, mandatory white paper disclosures for token issuers, and ongoing reporting obligations to national competent authorities. The regulation also establishes a passporting framework that allows authorised entities to operate across EU member states. For firms operating across MiCA and other jurisdictions, the challenge is maintaining continuous compliance across overlapping regulatory regimes with distinct reporting requirements.

Capabilities

Verification Infrastructure

CASP Asset Segregation (Art. 70)

Verification that client crypto assets are held separately from CASP proprietary holdings at all times. Continuous reconciliation between internal records and custodian or on-chain positions.

EMT Reserve Compliance (Title IV)

Monitoring of e-money token reserves to verify that backing assets meet MiCA's Title IV requirements. Reserve adequacy verification against circulating supply with qualifying asset classification.

ART Reserve Verification (Title III)

Reserve composition analysis for asset-referenced tokens, including diversification and liquidity requirements. Verification that reserve assets meet the specific criteria defined under MiCA Title III.

Regulatory Reporting

Structured outputs aligned to MiCA reporting obligations for national competent authorities. Reserve adequacy reports, segregation verification, and prudential status documentation.

Requirements

Key MiCA Requirements

Client Asset Segregation

Article 70 requires CASPs to hold client crypto assets and funds separately from their own at all times. Segregation must be verifiable through internal records and independent custodian confirmation.

Reserve Composition

EMT and ART issuers must maintain reserves composed of qualifying assets that meet specific liquidity, credit quality, and diversification criteria. The composition must be verified and reported on an ongoing basis.

Prudential Requirements

Authorised CASPs must maintain own funds not less than the higher of their permanent minimum capital requirement or one quarter of fixed overheads. Continuous monitoring ensures compliance with prudential thresholds.

Custody Obligations

CASPs providing custody and administration services must establish custody policies, maintain records of client positions, and ensure that client assets are protected in the event of insolvency.

White Paper Disclosures

Token issuers must publish and maintain a crypto-asset white paper containing prescribed disclosures. The data infrastructure supporting these disclosures must be accurate, current, and auditable.

Cross-Border Passporting

MiCA-authorised entities may passport services across EU member states. Compliance must be maintained at the home member state level while meeting any additional host member state notification requirements.
