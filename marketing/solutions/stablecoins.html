Stablecoin Reserve Verification

Continuous peg monitoring, reserve composition analysis, and regulatory attestation for stablecoin issuers.

Overview

The Reserve Verification Landscape

Stablecoin issuers face increasing regulatory pressure to demonstrate that circulating tokens are fully backed by qualifying reserve assets at all times. The EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation requires EMT and ART issuers to maintain verifiable reserves. The US GENIUS Act mandates dollar-for-dollar backing with narrow categories of qualifying assets. The UK FCA is developing its own stablecoin framework with similar reserve adequacy expectations.

Beyond reserve adequacy, regulators expect continuous peg stability monitoring, transparent reserve composition disclosure, and periodic attestation by independent parties. The operational challenge is that reserve assets span multiple custodians, asset classes, and jurisdictions. Issuers must reconcile on-chain supply data against off-chain reserve holdings and produce attestation-ready outputs that meet the specific requirements of each applicable regulatory regime.

Requirements

Key Regulatory Requirements

1:1 Reserve Backing

Stablecoin issuers must maintain reserve assets with a value equal to or exceeding the total circulating supply at all times. Any shortfall constitutes a regulatory breach requiring immediate remediation.

Qualifying Asset Verification

Reserve assets must fall within defined qualifying categories that vary by jurisdiction. These typically include cash, short-term government securities, and central bank deposits. Non-qualifying assets must be identified and excluded.

Peg Deviation Detection

Material deviations from the target peg value must be detected and reported. Regulators expect issuers to monitor peg stability continuously and to have documented procedures for responding to depeg events.

Monthly Attestation

Most regulatory frameworks require periodic attestation of reserve adequacy, typically on a monthly basis. Attestation reports must be produced by or supported by independent parties and made publicly available.

Redemption Compliance

Stablecoin holders must be able to redeem tokens at par value within specified timeframes. Issuers must maintain sufficient liquid reserves to meet redemption demand without delay or discount.

Issuer Prudential Requirements

Stablecoin issuers are subject to prudential requirements including minimum capital, governance arrangements, and risk management frameworks. These obligations vary by jurisdiction and issuer classification.
