Gaming

Player Fund Protection for Gaming Operators

Verification of player fund segregation for gambling operators licensed by the Gambling Commission.

The Gambling Commission requires all licensed operators to protect player funds through one of three recognised mechanisms: segregation of funds into a separate account, insurance arrangements, or placement into a trust. Operators must clearly disclose their level of player fund protection to customers.

Regardless of the protection method chosen, operators are expected to maintain systems and controls that prevent shortfalls between player balances and protected funds. The Gambling Commission assesses player fund protection as part of its licence condition compliance reviews, and failures can result in licence conditions, regulatory action, or revocation.

Safeheld provides automated verification infrastructure that enables gaming operators to demonstrate continuous compliance with player fund protection requirements — reconciling player balances against protected funds and generating the evidence trail that regulators expect.

Capabilities

What Safeheld Delivers

Player Fund Reconciliation

Automated matching of aggregate player balances against segregated bank accounts or trust holdings. Every player account balance is verified against the funds held in designated protection accounts.

Protection Level Monitoring

Continuous verification that the declared level of player fund protection is maintained at all times. The platform monitors protection arrangements against actual fund positions and flags any degradation.

Shortfall Detection

Immediate identification of any shortfall between total player balances and protected funds. Automatic severity classification and escalation workflows ensure rapid resolution before regulatory thresholds are breached.

Regulatory Reporting

Structured outputs for Gambling Commission compliance evidence and licence condition reporting. Complete data lineage from source records through to final report figures with timestamped audit trail.
