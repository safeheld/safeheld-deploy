CASS Compliance for Investment Firms

Automated client money and custody asset reconciliation under FCA CASS 6, CASS 7, and CASS 10.

Overview

The CASS Regulatory Framework

The FCA Client Assets sourcebook imposes daily reconciliation obligations on investment firms holding client money and custody assets. CASS 7.6 requires firms to perform an internal client money reconciliation on each business day, comparing client entitlements against records of money held. External reconciliation against third-party bank statements must also be conducted. For custody assets, CASS 6.6 mandates regular reconciliation between internal records and those of third-party custodians.

Beyond reconciliation, firms must submit the Client Money and Assets Return (CMAR) to the FCA, maintain a resolution pack for orderly wind-down scenarios, and operate within the CASS 10 governance framework. The CF10a function carries direct personal accountability for CASS compliance. Failures in any of these areas carry enforcement consequences and may result in client money audit qualifications.

Requirements

Key CASS Requirements

Daily Internal Reconciliation

CASS 7.6 mandates that firms reconcile client money balances internally on each business day. This involves comparing the total of individual client entitlements against the firm's record of client money held.

External Reconciliation

Firms must reconcile their own records of client money held against statements from banks and third parties where client money is deposited. Any discrepancies must be investigated and resolved promptly.

Client Money Calculation

The client money resource calculation determines whether the firm holds sufficient money in designated client accounts. Both the standard and alternative approach must account for all relevant adjustments.

Resolution Pack

CASS 10 requires firms to maintain a resolution pack containing sufficient information to facilitate the orderly distribution of client assets in the event of firm failure. This pack must be kept current.

CASS Operational Oversight

The CASS operational oversight function (CF10a) carries personal responsibility for the firm's compliance with CASS. The governance framework must document oversight arrangements and approval chains.

Audit and Examination Evidence

Firms must produce a complete audit trail supporting every reconciliation, calculation, and compliance decision. This evidence must be available for the annual CASS audit and FCA supervisory examination.
