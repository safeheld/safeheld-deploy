Client Money Protection for Law Firms

Automated verification of client account segregation under SRA Accounts Rules for solicitors and law firms.

The Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) Accounts Rules require law firms to keep client money separate from the firm's own money. Firms must maintain proper accounting records and perform regular reconciliations of client accounts.

Safeheld automates client account reconciliation, breach detection, and reporting for law firms — ensuring continuous compliance with SRA requirements.

Capabilities

What Safeheld Delivers

End-to-end client money compliance automation for law firms.

Client Account Reconciliation

Automated matching of client ledger against bank statements for all client accounts. Every balance is verified, every discrepancy is flagged and tracked to resolution.

SRA Compliance Monitoring

Continuous verification of compliance with SRA Accounts Rules. The platform monitors segregation requirements, accounting standards, and reconciliation schedules.

Breach Detection

Immediate identification when client money rules are breached. Automatic severity classification and escalation workflows ensure issues are addressed before they become regulatory failures.

Reporting & Audit Trail

Regulatory-grade outputs with complete audit evidence for SRA inspections. Every reconciliation event, data source, and calculation is recorded with full data lineage.

Key Requirements

SRA Accounts Rules Coverage

Safeheld addresses the core obligations that law firms must meet under SRA client money rules.

Segregation

Client money held separately from the firm's own funds in designated client accounts at all times.

Reconciliation

Regular comparison of client ledger balances against bank statements to verify accuracy and completeness.

Record Keeping

Complete, accurate accounting records maintained for all client money transactions and movements.

Reporting

Compliance returns and regulatory submissions prepared with full supporting evidence and audit trail.

Interest Rules

Proper handling of client interest in accordance with SRA requirements and firm policy obligations.

Residual Balances

Management of unclaimed balances with proper tracking, notification procedures, and resolution workflows.
