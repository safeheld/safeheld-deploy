Client Money Protection for Law Firms

Verification of client account segregation under SRA Accounts Rules for solicitors and regulated legal practices.

The Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) Accounts Rules impose strict obligations on law firms handling client money. Client funds must be kept entirely separate from the firm's own money at all times, with proper accounting records maintained for every transaction.

Firms are required to perform regular reconciliations of client account balances against bank statements, identify and resolve discrepancies without delay, and maintain a complete audit trail for regulatory inspection. The SRA conducts both routine and risk-based reviews of client money compliance, and failures can result in enforcement action, conditions on practising certificates, or intervention.

Safeheld provides automated verification infrastructure that enables law firms to demonstrate continuous compliance with these requirements — replacing manual reconciliation processes with systematic, auditable verification.
