Crypto Custody

Crypto Asset Custody Verification

On-chain wallet reconciliation, proof of reserves, and custody verification for crypto asset service providers.

Overview

The Crypto Custody Challenge

Crypto asset custody introduces verification challenges that do not exist in traditional finance. Client assets are distributed across hot wallets, cold storage vaults, and third-party custodians. Each custody arrangement involves different interfaces, confirmation mechanisms, and latency characteristics. On-chain balances can be verified independently, but mapping those balances to individual client entitlements requires purpose-built infrastructure.

The fragmented nature of crypto custody means that reconciliation must span blockchain data, custodian APIs, and internal ledger systems simultaneously. Proof of reserves requires cryptographic attestation that total on-chain holdings match or exceed aggregate client entitlements. As regulatory frameworks in the UK, EU, and US mature, the expectation for continuous, verifiable custody reporting is becoming a baseline requirement for licensed operators.

Capabilities

Verification Infrastructure

Wallet Reconciliation

Matching of internal client entitlement records against on-chain wallet balances across multiple blockchains and custodian platforms. Configurable reconciliation schedules with break detection.

Proof of Reserves

Cryptographically verifiable attestation that client assets are fully backed by on-chain holdings. SHA-256 hashed proof generation with complete data lineage from source through to output.

Hot/Cold Wallet Monitoring

Continuous monitoring of wallet balances with configurable alerts for unexpected movements, threshold breaches, and anomalous transaction patterns across all custody tiers.

Data Lineage

Full provenance tracking for every data point used in reconciliation and attestation. Every balance, transaction, and calculation is traceable from blockchain source through to compliance output.

Requirements

Key Custody Requirements

Asset Segregation

Client crypto assets must be held separately from the custodian's proprietary holdings. Segregation must be verifiable on-chain and through internal accounting records at all times.

Client Entitlement Matching

Each client's entitlement to crypto assets must be reconciled against the total holdings in custody. The aggregate of all client entitlements must not exceed verified on-chain balances.

Cryptographic Attestation

Proof of reserves must be generated using cryptographic methods that allow independent verification. Merkle tree proofs and signed attestations provide the evidence chain regulators expect.

Custody Arrangements

Where assets are held with third-party custodians, the firm must maintain documented custody arrangements and verify that the custodian's records agree with its own on a regular basis.

Multi-Chain Support

Custody operations span multiple blockchains and layer-2 networks. Verification infrastructure must reconcile across all chains where client assets are held, with consistent methodology and reporting.

Regulatory Reporting

Emerging regulatory frameworks require periodic reporting on custody arrangements, asset holdings, and segregation status. Structured outputs must be aligned to jurisdiction-specific requirements.
