Automated verification of client fund segregation for Electronic Money Institutions and Payment Institutions under UK FCA regulations.

Overview

The Safeguarding Challenge

Payment institutions hold client funds that must be safeguarded in accordance with the Payment Services Regulations 2017 and FCA guidance in PS 17/19. Manual reconciliation processes are error-prone and cannot scale.

Safeheld automates the entire safeguarding verification process — from data ingestion through reconciliation to regulatory reporting.

Regulatory Context

Regulatory Framework

Under the Payment Services Regulations 2017, payment institutions must safeguard relevant funds by the close of the business day following receipt. The FCA's PS 17/19 guidance sets expectations for segregation method, acknowledgement letters, and reconciliation frequency.

Firms using the segregation method must hold client funds in designated safeguarding accounts, perform daily reconciliation, and maintain records demonstrating compliance at all times. Failures in safeguarding can result in enforcement action, consumer harm, and reputational damage.

Safeheld is purpose-built to meet these requirements automatically. The platform enforces daily reconciliation schedules, monitors for segregation shortfalls in real time, and produces the evidence trail that regulators expect during supervisory review.
