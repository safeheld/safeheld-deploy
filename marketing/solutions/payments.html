Client Fund Safeguarding for Payment Institutions

Verification infrastructure for electronic money institutions and payment institutions under UK Payment Services Regulations.

Overview

The Safeguarding Landscape

The Payment Services Regulations 2017 require electronic money institutions and payment institutions to safeguard relevant funds by the close of the business day following receipt. The FCA's PS 17/19 guidance establishes detailed expectations for how firms implement the segregation method, including requirements for designated safeguarding accounts, acknowledgement letters from credit institutions, and the frequency and scope of reconciliation procedures.

Safeguarding obligations extend across internal record-keeping, external bank reconciliation, and regulatory reporting through the RMAR. The complexity of maintaining accurate, timely reconciliation across multiple banking relationships and product lines creates significant operational risk. Errors in segregation calculations, missed reconciliation deadlines, and incomplete audit trails are among the most common supervisory findings in this area.

Requirements

Key Regulatory Requirements

Segregation by Close of Business

Relevant funds must be placed in a designated safeguarding account by close of the business day following receipt. The segregation method requires funds to be held entirely separate from the firm's own assets.

Acknowledgement Letters

Credit institutions holding safeguarded funds must provide written acknowledgement that they have no recourse over client funds. These letters must be obtained and maintained for each safeguarding account.

Internal vs External Reconciliation

Firms must perform both internal reconciliation (matching client entitlements to internal records) and external reconciliation (matching internal records to bank statements) to verify the adequacy of safeguarded amounts.

RMAR Safeguarding Sections

Payment institutions must report safeguarding information through the Retail Mediation Activities Return. This includes details on safeguarded amounts, banking arrangements, and reconciliation status.

Adequate Safeguarding Calculation

The total amount held in safeguarding accounts must equal or exceed the total client fund liability at all times. The calculation methodology must account for in-flight transactions, fees, and timing differences.

Reconciliation Records

Firms must maintain detailed records of all reconciliation activity, including source data, matching results, identified breaks, and resolution actions taken. Records must be available for FCA supervisory review.
