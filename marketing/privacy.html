Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. Introduction

This policy describes how Safeheld Ltd ("Safeheld", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects personal data when you use the Safeheld platform and related services. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information.

Safeheld Ltd is the data controller for the purposes of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). We take our responsibilities as a data controller seriously and have implemented appropriate measures to ensure compliance with all applicable data protection legislation.

2. Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Account information: Name, email address, job title, organisation details, and authentication credentials
  • Usage data: Platform activity logs, feature usage patterns, session information, and access timestamps
  • Financial data: Client fund records, reconciliation data, and compliance information processed through the platform on behalf of your organisation
  • Communication records: Correspondence with our support team, feedback, and enquiry details
  • Technical data: IP addresses, browser type, device information, and operating system

We only collect personal data that is necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy. We do not collect sensitive personal data unless strictly required for regulatory compliance functions.

3. How We Use Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service delivery: To provide, maintain, and operate the Safeheld platform and its compliance features
  • Compliance monitoring: To enable automated reconciliation, breach detection, and regulatory reporting functions
  • Platform improvement: To analyse usage patterns, identify issues, and improve platform performance and features
  • Security: To detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, security incidents, and unauthorised access
  • Legal obligations: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes
  • Communication: To send service-related notifications, updates, and respond to your enquiries

4. Legal Basis

We process personal data on the following legal bases under the UK GDPR and GDPR:

  • Contract performance: Processing necessary to fulfil our contractual obligations to you and your organisation
  • Legitimate interests: Processing necessary for our legitimate business interests, including platform security, service improvement, and fraud prevention, where those interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms
  • Legal obligation: Processing necessary to comply with our legal and regulatory obligations
  • Consent: Where required, we will obtain your explicit consent before processing your personal data for specific purposes. You may withdraw consent at any time

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes.

Data may be shared with the following categories of recipients, only where necessary:

  • Cloud infrastructure providers: To host and operate the platform securely
  • Regulatory authorities: When legally required to comply with regulatory requests or court orders
  • Professional advisors: Legal, accounting, and audit professionals who are bound by professional confidentiality obligations

All third-party data processors are contractually bound to protect your data and process it only in accordance with our instructions. We conduct due diligence on all third-party processors and require them to maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures.

6. Data Security

We implement robust technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • AES-256 encryption: All data is encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard AES-256 encryption
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC): Platform access is restricted based on user roles, ensuring individuals can only access data relevant to their function
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA): MFA is available and encouraged for all platform accounts to provide an additional layer of security
  • Regular security assessments: We conduct periodic security audits, penetration testing, and vulnerability assessments
  • Audit logging: All platform actions are logged in an immutable audit trail for accountability and forensic analysis
  • Network security: Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and network segmentation protect the platform infrastructure

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data for the duration of your account relationship with Safeheld, plus any additional period required by applicable regulation or law. Specific retention periods vary depending on the type of data and the regulatory requirements that apply to your organisation.

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with our data retention policy. We review retention schedules regularly to ensure data is not held for longer than necessary.

8. Your Rights

Under the GDPR and UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Right to portability: Request a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
  • Right to restriction: Request that we limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to object: Object to the processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided below. We will respond to your request within one month, as required by law.

9. International Transfers

Data is primarily processed within the European Economic Area (EEA) and the United Kingdom. Any transfers of personal data outside the EEA are protected by appropriate safeguards, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission or the UK Information Commissioner's Office.

We will only transfer personal data to countries or organisations that provide an adequate level of data protection, or where appropriate safeguards are in place to ensure the security and privacy of your information.

10. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. Material changes will be communicated via the platform or by email. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was most recently revised.

11. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or wish to exercise your data protection rights, please contact us at privacy@safeheld.com.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In the UK, this is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). In the EU, you may contact the supervisory authority in your member state of residence.
