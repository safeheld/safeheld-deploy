Cookie Policy

Last updated: 1 March 2026

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function correctly, improve performance, provide a better browsing experience, and supply information to the site operators. Cookies may be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third parties whose services are integrated into the site ("third-party cookies").

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them, while session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. We use both types on our website and platform.

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage to collect and store information. References to "cookies" in this policy include these similar technologies unless otherwise stated.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies on the Safeheld website and platform for the following purposes:

Essential / Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are required for the operation of our website and platform. They enable core functionality such as page navigation, secure access to authenticated areas, session management, and load balancing. Without these cookies, the services you have requested cannot be provided. Essential cookies do not require your consent under applicable law, as they are strictly necessary for the provision of the service.

  • Session Cookie: Maintains your authenticated session while you are logged into the Safeheld platform. This cookie is deleted when you close your browser or when your session expires
  • CSRF Token: A security cookie that helps protect against cross-site request forgery attacks by verifying that form submissions and API requests originate from the Safeheld platform
  • Cookie Consent: Stores your cookie preference settings so that we can respect your choices on subsequent visits
  • Load Balancer Cookie: Ensures your requests are routed to the correct server for consistent performance during your session

Analytics Cookies

These cookies allow us to understand how visitors interact with our website and platform by collecting and reporting information about usage patterns. The data collected is aggregated and anonymised, meaning it cannot be used to identify you personally. Analytics cookies help us measure the number of visitors, identify which pages are most and least popular, and understand how visitors navigate through the site. This information enables us to improve the structure, content, and functionality of our website and platform.

  • Page View Tracking: Records which pages you visit, how long you spend on each page, and the order in which you navigate between pages
  • Performance Metrics: Collects data on page load times, error rates, and other performance indicators to help us identify and resolve issues
  • Feature Usage: Tracks which platform features are used most frequently, helping us prioritise development and improvements

Preference Cookies

Preference cookies enable our website and platform to remember choices you have made, such as your preferred language, display settings, timezone, or other customisation options. These cookies provide a more personalised and convenient experience by eliminating the need to reconfigure your settings each time you visit.

  • Display Preferences: Remembers your chosen theme, table layout preferences, and dashboard configuration within the Safeheld platform
  • Locale Settings: Stores your preferred language and regional formatting settings for dates, numbers, and currencies

3. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to the cookies we set directly, certain third-party services integrated into our website may set their own cookies. We carefully select our third-party partners and require them to comply with applicable data protection laws. However, we do not control the cookies set by third parties, and their use is governed by the respective third party's own cookie and privacy policies.

The third-party services that may set cookies on our website include:

  • Analytics Providers: We use analytics services to help us understand website traffic and usage patterns. These services may set cookies to distinguish unique visitors and track sessions
  • Content Delivery Networks (CDNs): We use CDN services to deliver static assets such as fonts, stylesheets, and scripts efficiently. CDN providers may set cookies for performance optimisation and security purposes
  • Customer Communication Tools: If we implement live chat or support widget functionality, the provider may set cookies to manage chat sessions and remember your conversation history

We do not use advertising or social media tracking cookies on our website. We do not share cookie data with advertising networks or data brokers.

4. Managing Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies (other than strictly necessary cookies). You can manage your cookie preferences in the following ways:

Browser Settings: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to block all cookies, block third-party cookies, accept all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set. The method for managing cookies varies between browsers; please refer to your browser's help documentation for specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and Site Permissions > Cookies and site data

Device Settings: On mobile devices, you can manage cookies and similar technologies through your device's privacy or browser settings.

Please note that blocking or deleting certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website and platform. If you disable essential cookies, you may not be able to access authenticated areas of the platform or use certain features. If you disable analytics cookies, we will not be able to collect usage data to improve our services, though your experience will not be otherwise affected.

For more information about cookies in general, including how to view and manage cookies stored on your device, you can visit www.allaboutcookies.org or www.aboutcookies.org.

When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies.

If we introduce new categories of cookies or materially change the way we use existing cookies, we will seek your consent where required by law before deploying such cookies.
